## Badge & Classification System ✅

### Three Badge Categories
- ✅ **🔥 Popular** - High engagement & positive sentiment (5+ net votes)
- ✅ **✓ Reproducible** - Multiple forks showing verification efforts (3+ forks)
- ✅ **⚡ Controversial but Replicable** - Contested yet rigorously tested (2+ downvotes AND 3+ forks)

### Badge Display & Filtering
- ✅ Display badges on hypothesis cards
- ✅ Filter by badge category
- ✅ All Ideas, Popular, Reproducible, Controversial filters
- ✅ Clear criteria descriptions

### Anti-Groupthink Design
- ✅ Controversial ideas don't disappear
- ✅ Only badges those being actively tested
- ✅ Shows both sides of debates
- ✅ Prevents echo chambers

## Meta-Analysis & Knowledge Synthesis ✅ NEW!

### Replication Tracking
- ✅ Submit replication results per hypothesis
- ✅ Track researcher name
- ✅ Record outcome (confirmed/failed)
- ✅ Document effect size (0-1 scale)
- ✅ Note boundary conditions and failure modes

### Automatic Synthesis at 20+ Replications
- ✅ **Replication Summary** - Success rate, counts, strength
- ✅ **Effect Size Analysis** - Mean effect with 95% confidence interval
- ✅ **Variance & Consistency** - SD, variance, heterogeneity (I²)
- ✅ **Failure Conditions** - Auto-extracted from failed replications
- ✅ **Strength Classification** - Very Weak to Very Strong

### Statistical Calculations
- ✅ Replication rate (% successful)
- ✅ Mean effect size (average across replications)
- ✅ 95% Confidence interval
- ✅ Standard deviation and variance
- ✅ Heterogeneity index (consistency metric)
- ✅ Failure condition synthesis

### Decision Support
- ✅ Strength of Evidence classification
- ✅ Color-coded visual indicators
- ✅ Ready-for-application guidance
- ✅ Research direction recommendations

## Use Case Examples

### 1. Researcher Wants to Replicate a Study
**Old Way:** Find paper → Design study → Conduct replications → Maybe publish  
**New Way:** Find hypothesis → Fork it → Report result → Auto-synthesis → Contribute to knowledge

### 2. Scientist Evaluates New Finding
**Old Way:** Read abstract → Assume it's true → Waste time if it doesn't replicate  
**New Way:** Check badges and meta-analysis → See 23 replications at 88% success → Proceed with confidence

### 3. Team Collaborates on Hypothesis
**Old Way:** Email, meetings, version control → Change history buried  
**New Way:** Shared hypothesis → Comment history → Tracked changes → Visible collaboration

### 4. Journal Club Discusses Results
**Old Way:** "We all read this paper..." (scattered opinions)  
**New Way:** View meta-analysis → See actual evidence → Evidence-based discussion

### 5. Forecaster Predicts Outcomes
**Old Way:** Make prediction, wait years for results, eventually check  
**New Way:** Lock prediction → Results submitted → Auto-score → Leaderboard updated

## Getting Started

### For Researchers Using the Platform
1. Visit http://localhost:3000/new
2. Create a hypothesis (or use a template)
3. Others will fork to test
4. Check /explore for voting and discussion
5. View /profile for your work

### For Hypothesis Creators
1. Post your hypothesis
2. Describe claim and method clearly
3. Invite collaboration via "🤝 Collaborate" button
4. Share findings in article section
5. Watch replications accumulate

### For Replication Scientists
1. Find a hypothesis to test on /explore
2. Click "Fork" to create your version
3. Conduct your replication
4. Go back to parent hypothesis
5. Click "📝 Report Replication Result"
6. Submit: outcome + effect size + notes
7. At 20+ replications, meta-analysis auto-appears
